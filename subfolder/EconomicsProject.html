National Income and Price Determination

Scott Sieger, Kyle Bowers, Michael Madden
Macroeconomics, Professor Greg Zogby, 2018

Real GDP and price levels are two economic indicators that hold a lot of weight when measuring a nation's output and overall well-being. In this project we look at how these indicators are measured, and how economic models show relationships and changes in the short run as well as the long run. We begin by looking at the Marginal Propensity to Consume and the Marginal Propensity to Spend. We then analyze the Consumption Function as well as the Aggregate Demand - Aggregate Supply curves in short run equillibrium to see how households and firms affect aggregate output and the price level. Economic policies and stabalizers are then discussed in an attempt to determine which course of action governments should take when facing a recession or slowing output. Keynesian thought is employed throughout the project.
